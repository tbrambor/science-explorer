The Life of Isaac Newton

Mathematician, physicist, astronomer, inventor, and one of history's greatest minds.

December 25, 1643
Born in Woolsthorpe, Lincolnshire, England on Christmas Day (using the old calendar). His father, also named Isaac, died three months before his birth.
1661–1665
Studied at Trinity College, Cambridge where he learned mathematics and natural philosophy (what we call physics).
1665–1666
Cambridge closed due to the Great Plague. Newton returned home and had his most productive years ever, inventing calculus, discovering gravity, and experimenting with light. He called this his "Year of Wonders" (annus mirabilis).
1666 (The Apple Story)
Legend says Newton was sitting under an apple tree in his garden when an apple fell on his head. He wondered: why do things fall down? This simple question led to his law of universal gravitation.
1687
Published Philosophiæ Naturalis Principia Mathematica (Mathematical Principles of Natural Philosophy) — the Principia. This 500-page book explained gravity, motion, and the solar system. It changed science forever.
1696
Became Master of the Royal Mint. He took the job seriously and personally caught counterfeiters and spies, sending many to be hanged. He was better at catching crooks than any law enforcer.
1705
Knighted by Queen Anne. He became Sir Isaac Newton — one of the highest honors in England. Few scientists have ever been knighted.
March 20, 1727
Died in London at age 84. He was given a state funeral and buried in Westminster Abbey — the same place as kings and queens. His tombstone says "Here rests all that was mortal of Isaac Newton."

Gravity Lab — Falling Objects

Newton discovered something amazing: all objects fall at the same rate (ignoring air resistance).

1
What is gravity? Gravity is a force that pulls all objects toward the Earth. It's the reason you don't float away and why the apple fell on Newton's head.
2
Acceleration: When you fall, gravity makes you go faster and faster. This speeding-up is called acceleration. On Earth, gravity accelerates all objects at 9.8 m/s².
3
All objects fall the same: A bowling ball and a feather fall at the same rate! Try this on the Moon where there's no air — they hit the ground together. Newton proved this with math.

Prism & Light — The Rainbow Secret

Newton proved that white light is actually all colors mixed together. A prism separates them.

1
White light is a mixture: Sunlight looks white, but it's actually made of ALL colors of the rainbow mixed together — red, orange, yellow, green, blue, indigo, and violet.
2
A prism separates colors: When white light hits a triangular piece of glass (a prism), each color bends at a slightly different angle. This splits the white light into a rainbow.
3
Newton's discovery: He proved this by passing white light through a prism, separating it into colors, then passing only ONE color (like red) through a second prism — it didn't separate further! This proved red light is pure, not a mix.

Calculus Taster — The Slope of a Curve

Newton invented calculus (with Leibniz) to find how fast things are changing. This is the core of calculus.

1
What is a slope? A slope tells you how fast something is changing. On a straight line, slope = rise ÷ run. Steep slope = fast change. Flat slope = slow change.
2
Curves are tricky: But what about curves? The slope is different at every point. Newton realized you can draw a tangent line (a line that just touches the curve) to find the slope at any point.
3
Calculus is born: Finding the slope of a curve at any point is called differentiation. It's one of the two main ideas of calculus and it's incredibly powerful for science and engineering.

Mind-Blowing Newton Facts

One man with incredible focus changed how we understand the universe.

🍎
The Apple Story (Maybe)
Newton said an apple fell from a tree and hit him, making him wonder why things fall. The story is probably exaggerated, but it's one of science's most famous moments. No apple was needed to invent gravity — just genius.
🎄
Born on Christmas (Old Calendar)
Newton was born on December 25, 1643, using the old Julian calendar. He was literally a Christmas baby! In the newer Gregorian calendar used today, his birthday is January 4, 1644.
🌾
Terrible Farmer
When Cambridge closed during the plague, Newton tried farming his family's land. He was so bad at it that his landlord eventually kicked him out. Good thing he stuck with math and physics!
🏛️
Silent Member of Parliament
Newton served as an MP (Member of Parliament) representing Cambridge University. But he barely spoke. Historical records show he only gave one speech during his entire time in Parliament — and it was to ask someone to close a window because of the draft.
🚔
Royal Mint Enforcer
Newton became Master of the Royal Mint and personally caught counterfeiters — criminals who made fake money. He was better at catching crooks than any police officer. He sent people to be hanged and took pride in his law enforcement record.
🐱
Invented the Cat Flap (Maybe)
People claim Newton invented the cat flap to let his cats move freely between rooms while he worked. It's probably not true, but it makes a great story. A genius who thought about everything — even his cats' convenience!
⚔️
Epic Rivalry with Leibniz
Newton and German mathematician Leibniz both invented calculus around the same time. Newton kept it secret in his notes. When Leibniz published first, Newton accused him of stealing. They argued for decades. Both deserved credit — Newton was just too secretive.
⚗️
Secret Alchemist
Newton wrote millions of words about alchemy (trying to turn lead into gold) — more than he wrote about physics! But his alchemy work was mostly dead-ends. His real genius showed in his scientific papers, not his mystical writings.
🌍
Predicted the World's End Date
Newton spent time studying ancient prophecies and calculated the world would not end before the year 2060. Pretty good prediction for 1704! Of course, science doesn't work that way — but it shows even geniuses can be odd.
👑
Knighted for Being Awesome
Queen Anne knighted Newton in 1705, making him Sir Isaac Newton. Scientists are rarely honored this way — it shows how important and famous he had become. His genius changed the world and everyone knew it.
📖
The Most Influential Science Book Ever
Newton's Principia is considered the most important science book ever written. It explained gravity, motion, and the entire solar system in mathematical detail. For 200 years it was the foundation of all physics.
🔐
Incredibly Secretive
Newton kept most of his discoveries secret for years, working alone and telling almost nobody. He published his gravity work 20 years after discovering it. If he'd been more open, science would have advanced even faster. Great ideas need to be shared!
