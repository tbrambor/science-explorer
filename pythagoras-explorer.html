The Life of Pythagoras

From brilliant student to the founder of a secret society obsessed with numbers.

570 BC
Born on the island of Samos in Greece. His father was a merchant, his mother encouraged his love of learning.
Age 12
Shows such exceptional talent that teachers encourage him to study with the best minds in Greece. Already fascinated by numbers and music.
Age 20-30
Travels to Egypt and Babylon to learn from priests and mathematicians. Spends years absorbing ancient wisdom about geometry and numbers.
Age 40
Settles in Croton, Italy and founds a secret society called the Pythagoreans. Members take vows of silence to keep discoveries secret!
Peak Years
The Pythagoreans become famous (and infamous) for mysterious rituals. They refuse to eat beans, believe numbers are alive, and keep mathematical proofs secret.
Age 50+
Proves the famous Pythagorean theorem and discovers the relationship between numbers and music—harmony has mathematical ratios!
Downfall
The discovery of irrational numbers (like √2) shocks the society—it contradicts the belief that "all is number" (whole numbers). Some say a Pythagorean was murdered for revealing this secret.
495 BC
Dies in exile, around age 75. But his ideas live forever—the Pythagorean theorem is still taught in every math class today!

Mind-Blowing Pythagoras Facts

This philosopher and mathematician was unlike anyone else.

📚
All is Number
Pythagoras believed numbers weren't just descriptions of nature—they were nature's actual essence. Everything is built from numbers!
🔐
Secret Society
The Pythagoreans were part math club, part religious cult, part super-secret society. Members took vows of silence and could be killed for revealing mathematical discoveries.
🫘
Bean Phobia
Pythagoreans refused to eat beans because they believed beans contained the souls of the dead. Some say Pythagoras died running through a bean field to escape attackers—he wouldn't cut through it!
🎵
Music & Math
Legend says Pythagoras discovered musical harmony by listening to blacksmith hammers. He proved that musical intervals are mathematical ratios—the first connection between sound and numbers!
⚡
The Pythagorean Crisis
Discovery of irrational numbers like √2 shocked the society—they couldn't be written as whole number ratios! This contradicted "all is number." The discovery was called "alogon" (unspeakable).
400+
Over 400 Proofs
The Pythagorean theorem has more than 400 different mathematical proofs! No other theorem in math has been proven so many different ways.
🏛️
Ancient Knowledge
Babylonians and Egyptians knew the theorem 1000+ years before Pythagoras! But he was the first to prove it mathematically, changing mathematics forever.
🇪🇬
Egyptian Pyramids
The ancient Egyptians used the 3-4-5 right triangle to make perfect right angles for building the pyramids. They marked rope with knots to measure exact distances!
👤
First Philosopher
Pythagoras may have been the first person to call himself a "philosopher"—lover of wisdom. The word was created to describe people like him!
🌟
President's Proof
James Garfield, who became a US President, discovered his own proof of the Pythagorean theorem in 1876 while serving in Congress! He's the only US President to publish a math proof.
✨
The Most Famous Theorem
The Pythagorean theorem is used everywhere—construction, navigation, astronomy, computer graphics, physics. It's been discovered independently by dozens of cultures around the world.
🎓
Still Teaching Today
Over 2,500 years later, the Pythagorean theorem is still a cornerstone of geometry taught in every high school. Few ideas have lasted so long or mattered so much.
