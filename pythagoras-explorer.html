Pythagoras: The Mystic Mathematician

One of history's most influential mathematicians and philosophers

c. 570 BC

Born on the Island of Samos

Pythagoras was born in ancient Greece on the island of Samos. His family was wealthy, which allowed him to travel and study widely.

Around 550 BC

Travels to Egypt and Babylon

Pythagoras left Samos and traveled to Egypt and Babylon, where he learned advanced mathematics, geometry, and astronomy from Egyptian priests and Babylonian scholars. He spent nearly 30 years there!

Around 530 BC

Founds the Pythagoreans in Croton

Pythagoras settled in Croton (southern Italy) and founded a secretive mathematical and philosophical society called the Pythagorean brotherhood. Members had to be initiated and kept strict vows of silence.

The Pythagorean Rules

Bizarre Beliefs and Customs

The Pythagoreans followed strange rules: no eating beans (they believed beans contained souls!), don't pick up fallen food, don't look at a mirror next to a candle, and never turn around to look back. Math mixed with mysticism!

Core Philosophy

"All is Number"

Pythagoras believed that numbers were the fundamental building blocks of the universe. Everything in nature—music, planets, light—could be explained through mathematical ratios and proportions.

Musical Discovery

The Mathematics of Harmony

Pythagoras discovered that musical notes that sound beautiful together have simple mathematical ratios (2:1, 3:2, etc.). Music is mathematics made audible! This was revolutionary—the first proof that nature follows mathematical laws.

Famous Discovery

The Pythagorean Theorem

The theorem a² + b² = c² made Pythagoras immortal in mathematics. It may not have been his original discovery (Babylonians knew it first), but he proved it and it changed geometry forever.

The Crisis

The Discovery of Irrational Numbers

A terrible problem: the Pythagoreans discovered that √2 (the hypotenuse of a 1-1 right triangle) cannot be expressed as a simple ratio of whole numbers. This broke their entire worldview! Legend says they drowned the member who revealed this secret.

c. 495 BC

Death and Legacy

Pythagoras died around 495 BC. His ideas influenced everyone who came after: Plato, Euclid, and virtually all of Western mathematics. He transformed how humans think about numbers and reality itself.

The Pythagorean Theorem

The Rule

In a right triangle (a triangle with one 90° angle), the square of the longest side (the hypotenuse, opposite the right angle) equals the sum of the squares of the other two sides.

a² + b² = c²

What's a Pythagorean Triple?

Some right triangles have sides that are ALL whole numbers! For example, a 3-4-5 triangle has whole number sides. These are called Pythagorean Triples. They're super useful in real life for construction and design.

The ancient mathematicians discovered a formula to generate infinite Pythagorean triples! Pick any two whole numbers m and n (where m > n), then:

a = m² - n²
b = 2mn
c = m² + n²

Music & Mathematics

Pythagoras's Amazing Discovery

Pythagoras discovered something that blew people's minds: musical harmony is based on simple mathematical ratios. When two notes have frequencies in a simple ratio (like 1:2, 2:3, or 3:4), they sound beautiful together. This was the first evidence that nature is fundamentally mathematical!

Legend says he figured this out listening to blacksmith hammers striking metal.

Musical Intervals and Their Ratios

Interval Name Frequency Ratio Description
Unison 1:1 Same note (frequencies identical)
Octave 2:1 The same note but higher. The higher note vibrates twice as fast.
Perfect Fifth 3:2 A very consonant interval (C to G). Used as the basis for many instruments!
Perfect Fourth 4:3 Another very consonant interval (C to F)
Major Third 5:4 A pleasant, major-sounding interval

Fun Facts About Pythagoras

Ancient Knowledge

The Pythagorean theorem was actually known in Babylon over 1000 years before Pythagoras! But he was the first to prove it mathematically.

Math Cult

The Pythagorean society was part math club, part religion, part mysterious cult. Members took vows of silence and kept their discoveries secret!

Bean Phobia

The Pythagoreans refused to eat beans because they believed beans contained the souls of the dead. They called beans "the children of the gods."

Deadly Secret

One legend says Pythagoras died because he refused to escape through a bean field during an attack. He chose death over breaking his rules!

Secret Society

Members who revealed Pythagorean secrets were supposedly drowned in the sea. Keeping quiet about mathematical discoveries was literally a life-or-death matter!

The Crisis

The discovery of irrational numbers (like √2) was so terrifying it was called the "alogon" or "unspeakable." It contradicted their philosophy that "all is number" (meaning whole numbers).

"All is Number"

Pythagoras believed numbers were literally the building blocks of reality itself. Not just descriptions of nature—but nature's actual essence!

Philosopher Inventor

Pythagoras may have been the first person to call himself a "philosopher" (lover of wisdom). The word was created to describe him!

400+ Proofs

The Pythagorean theorem has more than 400 different proofs! No other mathematical theorem is proved so many different ways.

Presidential Proof

James Garfield, who became a US president, discovered his own proof of the Pythagorean theorem in 1876 while serving in Congress!

Egyptian Geometry

The 3-4-5 triangle was used by ancient Egyptians to make perfect right angles for building the pyramids. They used rope knots to measure!

Blacksmith Story

Legend says Pythagoras discovered musical harmony by listening to blacksmith hammers of different weights. He connected sound and mathematics together!
